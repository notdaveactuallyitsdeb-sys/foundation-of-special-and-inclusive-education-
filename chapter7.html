Learners in Other Marginalized Groups

“Leaving no one behind must become a way of living.”

The final chapter really hit home and made me feel a bit heartbroken, but also very determined. Reading about children who are forced to work, children in war zones, or those who are abused was very difficult. It made me feel incredibly lucky for the life I have, but also responsible for those who haven't been as fortunate. It was a wake-up call that education is a luxury for many, even though it should be a right for everyone.

I learned that marginalization isn't just about disability; it’s about anyone who is pushed to the edges of society because of their circumstances. Whether it’s because of their culture, their poverty, or their safety, these children are often forgotten. I realized that as a future educator or just a citizen, I have to look beyond the classroom walls and care about the social issues that keep kids from learning and growing.

This chapter has inspired me to be more socially aware and active. It taught me that I can’t just ignore the problems of the world and hope they go away. I can apply this by supporting organizations that help children in need or by simply being a voice for those who are often ignored. It has made me more grateful, more humble, and more committed to making sure that leaving no one behind is more than just a phrase—it’s a goal I want to live by.
