About the Author

A journey of becoming a future educator

Dave

My name is Dave, a proud 18-year-old resident of Barangay Santa Ana in La Paz, Leyte. I am currently a first-year college student taking up the Bachelor of Secondary Education, majoring in Science (BSED Science).

Living in a small, close-knit community has taught me the value of helping others and staying connected to my roots. As I start my journey in higher education, I am fueled by a strong desire to learn and a dream to one day give back to my hometown through the power of teaching.

Ever since I chose this path, my goal has been to become a teacher who does more than just explain science experiments. I want to be a mentor who inspires curiosity and makes every student feel welcome in the classroom.

Being a future educator means I have a big responsibility to shape the minds of the next generation. I am committed to improving myself every day by studying hard and practicing patience, so that when I finally stand in front of a class, I can lead with both knowledge and a kind heart.

Studying foundations of special and inclusive education has deeply changed the way I see my future profession. I now understand that a true classroom is one where every learner—regardless of their abilities or background—is given a fair chance to succeed. This knowledge has made me more compassionate and has shown me that the heart of teaching is making sure every student feels they belong.
