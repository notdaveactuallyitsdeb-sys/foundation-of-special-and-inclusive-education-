Understanding Diversity

“Being different is actually the most natural thing in the world.”

When I first started reading about diversity, I felt a bit overwhelmed by how different we all really are. It made me realize that I often group people together without thinking, but this chapter reminded me that every single person has their own story and their own set of colors. It’s a bit scary to think about how much I don’t know about the people around me, but it’s also beautiful to see that our differences aren’t things that should divide us, but things that make the world more interesting.

I learned that diversity isn't just about what I can see on the outside, like skin color or age, but also the things hidden deep inside, like someone’s beliefs or their personal struggles. The "Diversity Wheel" really opened my eyes to how many layers we all have. I realized that being "different" is actually the most natural thing in the world. It’s not a problem to be solved; it’s just the way we were made, and that deserves a lot of respect.

This chapter changed how I look at my friends and even strangers. From now on, I want to be more patient and kind because I never know what "invisible" traits someone might be carrying. I want to be the kind of person who makes others feel safe being exactly who they are. In my future classroom or even just in my daily life, I will try to look past the surface and appreciate the unique "mix" that every person brings to the table.
