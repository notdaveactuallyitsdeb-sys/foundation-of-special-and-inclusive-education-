Learners with Additional Needs

“It’s about seeing what people can do, not only what they cannot.”

Reading this chapter was a very emotional experience for me. Learning about the specific challenges that students face—like difficulty seeing, hearing, or focusing—made me feel a lot of empathy. It’s easy to take things like reading a book or sitting still for granted, and this chapter reminded me that for some, these are daily battles. It made me feel a strong desire to be a more supportive friend and a more inclusive person.

I learned so much about the different categories of needs, from giftedness to learning disabilities. One thing that really stuck with me is that having a "difficulty" in one area doesn't mean a person isn't smart or capable. I realized that students with additional needs often have incredible strengths in other areas, and it’s our job to find those strengths and help them shine. It’s about looking at what they can do, not just what they can’t.

This chapter affected me by making me more aware of the barriers people face every day. In real life, I will try to be more mindful of how I communicate and how I share information. For example, if I'm working in a group, I’ll check if everyone can see the materials or understand the instructions. I want to be the kind of person who proactively looks for ways to make things easier for others, ensuring that everyone feels included and capable.
