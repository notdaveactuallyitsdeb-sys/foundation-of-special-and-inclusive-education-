Making Schools Inclusive

“A school should feel like a place where everyone belongs.”

This chapter made me feel very hopeful and inspired. Sometimes, the idea of inclusion feels like just a fancy word, but reading about how to actually make a school inclusive made it feel possible. It gave me a warm feeling to imagine a school where every child, no matter their background, feels like they truly belong. It made me wish that every school I attended had these kinds of values at their heart.

I learned that making a school inclusive isn't just about one teacher or one classroom; it’s about the whole culture of the building. I really liked the idea of Universal Design for Learning (UDL), which showed me that there are so many creative ways to teach so that no one gets bored or left behind. I realized that if we change the way we teach, we can unlock the potential of students who might have otherwise given up on themselves.

Personally, this chapter taught me that I need to be more flexible in how I help others. I can apply this by remembering that there isn't just one right way" to learn or do something. If I'm ever in a position to lead or teach, I will make sure to offer different choices and paths for people to succeed. It’s about being creative and caring enough to make sure everyone can follow along and participate.
